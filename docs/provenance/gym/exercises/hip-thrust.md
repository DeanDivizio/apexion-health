## `hipThrust` evidence

Defaults location: `lib/gym/exercises.ts`

### Foot angle (toes out / external rotation) → glute max

- **Code pointer**: `hipThrust` → `variationEffects.footAngle.toesOut`
- **What it supports**: “rotation hip thrust” / external rotation intent or toes-out positioning can increase **gluteus maximus** EMG vs traditional hip thrust.
- **Sources**
  - Systematic review summary (includes foot-position variations study): `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6544005/`
  - Hip thrust variations paper (abstract): `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30335717/`
- **Confidence**: Medium.

### Knee angle (proxy for foot placement distance) → hamstrings vs glutes

- **Code pointer**: `hipThrust` → `variationEffects.kneeAngle`
- **What it supports**:
  - feet farther (less knee flexion / more “extended” knee angle) tends to increase **hamstring** involvement
  - feet closer (more knee flexion / “flexed” knee angle) biases away from hamstrings toward glutes
- **Sources**
  - Hip thrust foot-position variations summary: `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6544005/`
  - Hip thrust variations paper (abstract): `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30335717/`
  - Joint-position torque contribution framing (not hip thrust-specific, but supports the knee-angle mechanism): `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34496710/`
- **Confidence**: Medium for direction; Low for exact magnitudes.

### Notes / gaps

- We have **limited direct evidence** isolating **stance width** effects specifically for barbell hip thrust EMG.
  Stance-width deltas should remain conservative until we have a better study.
