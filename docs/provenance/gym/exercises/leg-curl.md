## `legCurl` evidence

Defaults location: `lib/gym/exercises.ts`

### Base targets (hamstrings)

- **Code pointer**: `legCurl.baseTargets`
- **Rationale**: Knee flexion is primarily hamstrings (plus some gastrocnemius), tracked here as `hamstrings`.
- **Confidence**: High.

### Body position (seated vs prone) / long vs short muscle length

- **Code pointer**: `legCurl.variationEffects.bodyPosition`
- **What it supports**: Training hamstrings at longer muscle lengths (often approximated by seated leg curl vs prone leg curl) can produce greater hypertrophy in bi-articular hamstrings in some protocols.
- **Sources**
  - Long vs short muscle length hamstring training (Mäeo et al.): `https://ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7969179/`
- **Confidence**: Medium for hypertrophy differences; Low for translating to precise “muscle contribution deltas”.

### Foot angle / cadence / pause

- **Code pointers**: `legCurl.variationEffects.footAngle`, `.cadence`, `.pause`
- **Notes**: Effects are minor or uncertain at our granularity; defaults remain neutral.
- **Confidence**: Low.
